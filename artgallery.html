About the Collective

We Theertha International
Art That
Matters

Theertha International was founded in 2018 by a group of artists who believed contemporary art deserved a permanent home — not just in museums, but in the cultural memory of our time. We are archivists, curators, and creators in equal measure.

The Theertha International Collective emerged from a series of informal conversations held in a warehouse studio in the Meatpacking District. What began as a desire to document the overlooked — the temporary, the site-specific, the deliberately impermanent — evolved into a fully realized institution housing over 120 works across multiple disciplines.

We believe that art should be accessible without being diluted. That preservation is itself a form of creation. That the Theertha International is not a graveyard, but a living body — pulsing, growing, breathing with the work it holds. Our physical space spans 8,000 square feet of flexible gallery rooms, artist studios, and a reading library.

Today, Theertha International represents 34 artists from 19 countries, spanning painting, sculpture, photography, video, performance, and digital media. We operate on a non-profit model, with all acquisition funding directed transparently to artist compensation and program development.

Our Values
01
Preservation
Every work we acquire is documented, conserved, and made permanently accessible.

Equity
We prioritize artists from underrepresented backgrounds and work against systemic inequities.
03
Dialogue
We create space for critical conversation between artists, curators, and audiences.
04
Risk
We invest in work that challenges, disrupts, and refuses easy classification.
